Process Flow Overview

Sulfur is combusted at 900–1100°C producing SO₂ gas. The gas is cooled and dried before entering a multi-bed catalytic converter containing V₂O₅ catalyst where SO₂ is oxidized to SO₃. The SO₃ is absorbed in concentrated sulfuric acid forming oleum, which is subsequently diluted to obtain commercial H₂SO₄.

Technical Engineering Details

Sulfur Burning Furnace

Highly exothermic reaction: S + O₂ → SO₂. Operates at 900–1100°C under slight positive pressure. Moisture presence may cause downstream acid mist formation.

Catalytic Converter

Reversible exothermic reaction: 2SO₂ + O₂ ⇌ 2SO₃. Operates at 420–450°C using vanadium(V) oxide catalyst. Catalyst inventory: 120 tons, replaced every 3–4 years.

Absorption Tower

SO₃ absorbed in concentrated H₂SO₄ forming oleum. Direct water absorption avoided due to acid mist formation.

Dilution Section

Strongly exothermic hydration reaction. Cooling required to prevent overheating and aerosol formation.
